# Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a common form of cybercrime in which attackers impersonate trusted organizations to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, OTPs, or banking details.

A typical phishing incident targeting students begins with a message or email that appears to be from a legitimate source such as a bank, UPI app, or even a university portal. The message usually creates a sense of urgency, for example stating that the user’s account will be suspended, a payment has failed, or verification is required immediately. It contains a link that redirects the user to a fake website designed to closely resemble the original platform.

Once the student clicks the link and enters their login credentials or OTP, the information is instantly captured by the attacker. In many cases, attackers use this data to gain unauthorized access to bank accounts or digital wallets and perform fraudulent transactions within minutes.

Students are frequent targets because they rely heavily on digital platforms and may not always verify the authenticity of messages. The consequences of such attacks can include financial loss, identity theft, and compromise of personal, academic, or social media accounts.

This case highlights the importance of being cautious while clicking links, verifying the sender’s authenticity, and never sharing confidential information online without proper verification.
